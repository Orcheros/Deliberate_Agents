# Video Hook Archetypes

## Short-Form Hooks (first 3 seconds)

### 1. Outcome First
Pattern: Start with the end result
Example: "This one change doubled our conversion rate." [then explain]
Best for: Tutorials, tips, results

### 2. Pattern Interrupt
Pattern: Start with something unexpected (visual or verbal)
Example: [Upside-down camera] "Everything you know about pricing is backwards."
Best for: Attention in crowded feeds

### 3. Direct Challenge
Pattern: Call out a common mistake immediately
Example: "Stop doing this with your landing page."
Best for: Advice content, contrarian takes

### 4. Curiosity Gap
Pattern: Tease the payoff without revealing it
Example: "The reason your demo calls fail has nothing to do with your product."
Best for: Storytelling, longer shorts

### 5. Social Proof
Pattern: Start with a credibility marker
Example: "After 500 customer interviews, here's the one thing that matters."
Best for: Authority building, research shares

## Long-Form Hooks (first 10 seconds)

### 1. Cold Open
Pattern: Jump into the most interesting moment, then rewind
Example: "—and that's when the entire database went down. Let me back up."
Best for: Story-driven content, documentaries

### 2. Big Question
Pattern: Pose the question the entire video answers
Example: "Why do 90% of SaaS companies never pass $1M ARR?"
Best for: Educational, analytical content

### 3. Preview Stack
Pattern: Show 3-4 things they'll learn
Example: "In this video: why MRR lies to you, what to track instead, and how we fixed it."
Best for: Value-packed tutorials, frameworks

### 4. Contrarian Opening
Pattern: State something that goes against conventional wisdom
Example: "Product-market fit is a myth. Here's what actually matters."
Best for: Thought leadership, debate-style content

## Selection Guide
- Teaching something → Outcome First or Preview Stack
- Telling a story → Cold Open or Curiosity Gap
- Sharing an opinion → Direct Challenge or Contrarian Opening
- Showing results → Social Proof or Outcome First
