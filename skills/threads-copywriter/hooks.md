# Threads Hook Archetypes

## 1. Hot Take
Pattern: "[Opinion stated as fact]"
Example: "Nobody actually reads those 2000-word blog posts they bookmark"
Best for: Relatable observations, industry commentary

## 2. Confession
Pattern: "Honestly, [thing you shouldn't admit]"
Example: "Honestly, I still google 'how to center a div' at least once a month"
Best for: Relatability, vulnerability, humor

## 3. Observation
Pattern: "[Thing everyone does/thinks but doesn't say]"
Example: "The gap between 'I should post more' and actually posting is about 6 months"
Best for: Community bonding, shared experiences

## 4. Question (Genuine)
Pattern: "[Actual question you're curious about]"
Example: "What's something you stopped doing that made your work better?"
Best for: Starting conversations, genuine engagement

## 5. Reaction
Pattern: "[Brief reaction to something happening]"
Example: "Just watched a startup raise $50M with a landing page and a waitlist. We're all in the wrong business."
Best for: Timely commentary, humor

## 6. Micro-Lesson
Pattern: "[One specific thing I learned] in [context]"
Example: "Learned the hard way: the feature your loudest user wants is never the one that grows revenue"
Best for: Quick insights, experience sharing

## Selection Guide
- Funny/relatable → Hot Take or Confession
- Thoughtful → Observation or Question
- Timely → Reaction
- Useful → Micro-Lesson
