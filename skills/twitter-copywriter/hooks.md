# Twitter Hook Archetypes

## 1. Contrarian Statement
Pattern: "[Common belief] is wrong. Here's why:"
Example: "Your morning routine doesn't matter. Your decision-making system does."
Best for: Thought leadership, strong opinions

## 2. Surprising Data
Pattern: "[Specific number/stat] that changes how you think about [topic]"
Example: "93% of startups that raised Series A in 2024 had exactly 0 paying customers at seed."
Best for: Research findings, industry insights

## 3. Micro-Story
Pattern: "[Character] did [unexpected thing]. Result: [outcome]"
Example: "Our best engineer deleted 4,000 lines of code on Friday. Revenue went up 12% Monday."
Best for: Lessons learned, case studies

## 4. Pattern Recognition
Pattern: "Every [category] I've seen [succeed/fail] has [trait]"
Example: "Every dev tool that wins has one thing: it's faster to learn than to Google the alternative."
Best for: Expertise demonstration, frameworks

## 5. Bold Prediction
Pattern: "In [timeframe], [specific change]. Here's what that means:"
Example: "By 2027, the median SaaS company will have more AI agents than human employees."
Best for: Thought leadership, trend commentary

## 6. Direct Challenge
Pattern: "Stop [common behavior]. Start [alternative]."
Example: "Stop optimizing your landing page. Start talking to the 5 people who didn't buy."
Best for: Actionable advice, behavior change

## 7. Confession/Vulnerability
Pattern: "I [admission that goes against expectations]"
Example: "I run a SaaS company and I still use spreadsheets for half my processes."
Best for: Relatability, trust building

## 8. One-Liner Insight
Pattern: Single sentence that reframes something obvious
Example: "The best product roadmap is a list of things you decided NOT to build."
Best for: Quotable moments, viral potential

## Selection Guide
- Topic has data? → Surprising Data or Pattern Recognition
- Topic is an opinion? → Contrarian Statement or Bold Prediction
- Topic is a story? → Micro-Story or Confession
- Topic is advice? → Direct Challenge or One-Liner
